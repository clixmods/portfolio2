# Terra Memoria

## Project Description

> *"Terra Memoria takes place in the world of Terra, where a shortage of magical crystals and the sudden awakening of ancient machines push six friends to lead an extraordinary investigation."*

**Terra Memoria** is a cozy RPG developed in Unity by the **independent studio La Moutarde** and published by **Dear Villagers**. Blending pixel art and low-poly 3D, it offers an adventure focused on exploration, storytelling, and crafting, in a colorful and warm universe. The game was released on **March 27, 2024** on PC (Steam & Epic Games Store), Nintendo Switch, Xbox Series S/X, and PlayStation 5.

## My Contributions

Participation in the end of production of the RPG and its post-release maintenance. First immersion in a complete professional project, under real conditions, with quality requirements and multi-platform compliance.

### Maintenance & User Experience

- **Critical bug fixes**: softlocks, save file corruption, memory crashes on Switch
- **Internal script anomaly resolution**
- **Menu ergonomic optimization**: better mouse support, integration of interactive buttons
- **Internal tool production**: e.g., PDF export of game guide for merchandise creation

### Console Porting & Epic Games Integration

- **Work on Nintendo Switch porting**: CPU/GPU analysis, loading time optimization, critical crash resolution
- **Epic Games Store integration**: achievements, cloud saves, access token management
- **Contribution to multi-platform tracking** with internal tools

### Organization & Quality Process

- **Continuous integration tracking** of fixes at the end of the production cycle
- **Use of studio QA workflows**: User Report, ClickUp, technical discussions
- **Close collaboration with testers / QA team**: feedback on bug reports, scenario reproduction, fix validation and retests

## Results

This project gave me invaluable experience in commercial video game development: from the end of production to post-release maintenance, through console porting and multi-platform integration. A total immersion in the professional processes of a development studio in collaboration with a publisher (Dear Villagers).
